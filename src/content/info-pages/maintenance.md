Our handcrafted metalware is built to last generations. With proper care, it only gets more beautiful with time.

## Copper Items

Copper develops a natural patina over time — this is a sign of authenticity.

### Cleaning

1. Mix **lemon juice + salt** (or vinegar + salt)
2. Rub gently with a soft cloth in circular motions
3. Rinse with warm water
4. **Dry immediately** to prevent water spots

### Polishing

- Use a commercial copper polish for best results
- For natural shine: rub with a soft cloth and ketchup for 5 minutes
- Rinse and dry thoroughly

### Storage

- Store in a dry, ventilated area
- Wrap in soft cloth to prevent scratches
- Avoid plastic bags — they trap moisture

## Brass Items

Brass develops a warm, golden patina that many find beautiful.

### Cleaning

1. Make a paste with **baking soda + water**
2. Apply with a soft cloth
3. Rub gently, then rinse with warm water
4. Dry immediately

### Polishing

- Use brass-specific polish for high shine
- Apply thin layer of **mineral oil** after polishing to protect

### Avoid

- Abrasive cleaners or steel wool
- Dishwasher (hand wash only)
- Prolonged water contact

## Bronze Items

Bronze is the most durable and develops a rich patina.

### Cleaning

1. Use **mild soap + warm water**
2. Soft cloth only — no abrasives
3. Rinse and dry immediately

### Polishing

- Use bronze-specific polish compound
- Follow with a protective wax coating

### Avoid

- Harsh chemicals (bleach, ammonia)
- Direct sunlight during drying

## General Care Tips

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Hand wash with mild soap | Use dishwasher |
| Dry immediately after washing | Air dry (causes water spots) |
| Store in cool, dry place | Store near heat or humidity |
| Use soft cloths only | Use abrasive scrubbers |
| Apply protective wax seasonally | Neglect regular maintenance |

## Natural Patina

Over time, copper and bronze develop a **natural patina** — a beautiful coating that protects the metal. This is normal and desirable.

**Want to keep the shine?** Follow polishing instructions and apply paste wax seasonally.

**Want to encourage patina?** Leave the item exposed to air. The patina develops naturally over weeks.
